Executive Summary

Scheme 1 (All-Buck) offers the highest efficiency and best thermal performance at 85°C, critical for high-current rails, but at the highest cost ($26.2k). Scheme 2 (Hybrid Buck+LDO) is the most cost-effective ($10.42k) but suffers significant efficiency losses and thermal stress due to LDO dropout (e.g., 12V to 0.85V via 3.3V rail) at high ambient temperatures. Scheme 3 (Thermal Balanced) provides a middle ground ($21.6k) by grouping low-voltage rails into multi-output µModules to reduce component count and improve heat spreading, though it is slightly more expensive than Scheme 1 due to the premium LTM4675. Scheme 1 is recommended for high-reliability industrial applications, while Scheme 2 suits cost-sensitive, lower-power constraints if thermal limits are managed.
