Executive Summary

Scheme 1 (All-Buck) offers the highest efficiency (90% target) and best thermal performance at 85°C by avoiding LDO dropout losses, but at the highest cost ($16,200). Scheme 2 (Hybrid) is the most cost-effective ($7,420) by using LDOs for low-current rails fed from Buck outputs, though it introduces thermal risks for LDOs with high dropout voltages (e.g., 0.85V rail from 5V input). Scheme 3 (Thermal Optimized) balances cost ($13,200) and thermal safety by using high-power Buck modules for heavy loads and feeding LDOs from the 3.3V rail to minimize LDO power dissipation, ensuring robust operation at 85°C ambient.
