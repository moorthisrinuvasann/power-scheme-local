Executive Summary

Scheme 1 offers maximum thermal robustness and efficiency by using dedicated Buck converters for all rails, eliminating LDO dropout losses and heat generation at 85°C ambient, but at the highest cost (~$45,300). Scheme 2 is the most cost-effective (~$20,480) by utilizing LDOs for low-current low-voltage rails (V3, V6, V7) fed from the V1 rail, though this introduces thermal risks at high ambient temperatures and reduces overall efficiency. Scheme 3 balances performance and cost (~$34,000) by using Buck converters for all rails to strictly meet PSRR and ripple requirements while maintaining high thermal margins, avoiding the efficiency penalties of LDOs without the full component redundancy of Scheme 1.
